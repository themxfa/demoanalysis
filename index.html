Hairstyle Suggestion

Textured Crop Fade

A textured crop fade would suit your features really well, since it adds structure on top while keeping things clean and tight on the sides. The sides and back are faded down gradually with clippers, getting progressively shorter toward the skin, while the top is left longer at around two to three inches to allow for natural movement and texture. Style it forward or slightly to the side using a matte paste or clay for a soft, lived-in finish rather than a slick or shiny look, and avoid heavy gels that can flatten the texture you're going for. This cut is incredibly low-maintenance day to day, requiring just a quick scrunch or finger-comb in the morning, but it also cleans up nicely with minimal effort for sharper occasions, making it one of the most versatile options for your face shape and hair type.

Outfit Suggestion

Sharp & Versatile

Your build and proportions respond really well to structured, well-fitted pieces rather than oversized or baggy silhouettes, since clean lines help balance your frame and give a more put-together impression at a glance. A tailored bomber jacket layered over a plain crewneck tee is an easy way to instantly elevate a casual outfit without trying too hard, and it works across seasons when paired correctly. On the bottom, slim-fit chinos or dark denim create a streamlined look that pairs nicely with the jacket, and finishing with minimalist leather sneakers in white, black, or tan keeps the overall palette cohesive and modern. For colder days, swap the tee for a fine-knit turtleneck under the jacket to add a touch of refinement, and consider a structured watch or a simple leather belt as small details that tie the whole outfit together without overcomplicating it.

Custom Skincare Routine

Hydration Focused

Your skin appears to have a combination profile, meaning some areas like the forehead and nose may produce more oil while the lower cheeks show slight signs of dehydration and tightness, especially after washing. Start your morning routine with a gentle hydrating cleanser that won't strip your skin's natural oils, followed by a Vitamin C serum to help brighten the complexion and even out any unevenness in tone over time. Once the serum has absorbed, apply a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer to lock in hydration without feeling heavy, and always finish with a broad-spectrum SPF 50 sunscreen to protect against sun damage and premature aging. In the evening, repeat the cleansing step and consider introducing a gentle exfoliant two to three times a week to keep your pores clear and your skin texture smooth and even.

Colors & Style

Deep & Bold Contrast

Your cool undertones and higher contrast features mean rich, saturated colors will generally look better on you than washed-out pastels, which can sometimes make your complexion appear flat or dull by comparison. Build the foundation of your wardrobe around deep navy blues, emerald greens, and charcoal grays, as these jewel tones create a striking contrast against your skin and eyes while still being easy to mix and match across different outfits. Accent pieces in burgundy, deep purple, or icy blue can add variety without clashing, and metals like silver or platinum will generally suit you better than warm gold tones for jewelry or watch accents. When in doubt, darker and more saturated versions of any color will almost always be more flattering on you than lighter, muted, or pastel variations.

Grooming Advice

Emphasizing The Jawline

Keeping your facial hair trimmed to a neat 3 to 5mm stubble is one of the easiest ways to enhance your jawline, since it adds a subtle shadow that emphasizes definition without covering up your strong natural bone structure underneath. Use a quality trimmer with a guard to maintain an even length across your cheeks, jaw, and neckline, and clean up any stray hairs along your cheekbones and neck every few days to keep the shape sharp and intentional rather than patchy or unkempt. Apply a few drops of beard oil daily after washing your face, massaging it into the skin underneath to prevent dryness, itchiness, and flaking, which is especially important if you're maintaining a shorter length. If you ever decide to grow it out slightly longer, make sure to brush it daily to train the direction of growth and keep it looking deliberate rather than messy.
